Our Services 服務項目

Quan Zhen Cultural Society offers ritual services rooted in the Quanzhen Longmen tradition, alongside educational programmes for members and the wider public — open to all, regardless of prior practice. 全真教化協會提供植根於全真龍門道統的科儀服務，以及面向會員與公眾的教育課程 — 歡迎各界人士參與，不論是否曾有修道經驗。

Rites and ceremonies科儀與禮斗

Ritual Services科儀服務

科儀服務

Our Society provides the following rituals and prayers to our members, and to anyone who requires our assistance. 本會為會員及任何有需要人士，提供以下科儀及祈福服務。

Our Ritual Team is led by six High Priests, "Gao Gong," supported by over 30 ordained Taoist priests. Key rituals are presided over by our High Priests; other rituals may be led by an ordained Taoist priest. 本會科儀團隊由六位高功法師帶領，並由超過三十位受度道長協助。重要科儀由高功法師主持，其他科儀則由受度道長主理。

Blessing Rituals祈福科儀
  1. Lidou prayers to pray for health (北斗禮斗科儀, monthly)北斗禮斗科儀祈求健康 (每月舉行)
  2. Pre-surgery prayers手術前祈福
  3. Ground-breaking rituals for construction sites or projects動土科儀（工程或建築項目）
  4. Consecration "Kai Guang" rituals for new statues or altars開光科儀（神像或神壇）
  5. Blessing of homes or workplaces居所或工作場所祈福
  6. Prayers at locations affected by suicide or unnatural deaths非自然死亡地點超度祈福
  7. Interfaith prayers to bless new sites跨宗教祈福儀式（新場地）
  8. Other rituals or prayers其他科儀或祈福服務
Salvation Rituals超度科儀
  1. Prayers at wakes, before funeral (members only, upon request for loved ones)喪禮前守靈祈福 (僅限會員為其親屬申請)
  2. "Gong-de" salvation rituals for the deceased功德超度法會（為亡者）
  3. Salvation rituals at fatal accident locations意外事故地點超度科儀
  4. Other rituals or prayers其他科儀或祈福服務

We do charge a fee for our ritual services to support our activities. We thank you for your kind support! 本會科儀服務會收取一定費用，以支持會務運作，感謝您的體諒與支持！

For the underprivileged and destitute (or any special case), kindly contact us for assistance — charges will be waived or reduced. 如屬經濟困難、貧困或特殊個案，歡迎聯絡本會尋求協助，費用可獲豁免或減免。

Annual rites observed during the seventh lunar month, honouring ancestors and offering blessings to wandering spirits. 於農曆七月舉行的年度科儀，緬懷先人並超度孤魂。

太歲科儀 · Taisui Prayers太歲科儀

Prayers to the Taisui (太歲) — the presiding deity of the year — observed at two points in the year. At Lunar New Year, members petition the incoming Taisui for protection and blessings (拜太歲), particularly if their zodiac sign is in conflict with the year (犯太歲). Before Winter Solstice, members return to give thanks (謝太歲) and formally close the year's cycle of protection. 祈求太歲庇佑之科儀，一年舉行兩次。農曆新年期間，信眾可拜太歲，祈求來年平安順遂，尤其適合生肖犯太歲者；冬至前則舉行謝太歲科儀，答謝太歲一年庇佑，圓滿結束該年的護佑週期。

Lunar New Year — 拜太歲 (petition)農曆新年 — 拜太歲 Before Winter Solstice — 謝太歲 (thanksgiving)冬至前 — 謝太歲
